Most RDKit usage in small teams starts as a notebook cell that “just works once.” Six months later nobody remembers which salt-stripping step was required, or why one library failed silently.

## A durable pattern

A reusable cheminformatics workflow is usually four stages:

1. **Ingest** — SMILES, SDF, or mixed formats into one internal representation  
2. **Standardize** — RDKit cleanup rules the team agrees on  
3. **Compute** — descriptors, fingerprints, or filters  
4. **Export** — tables and structure files other tools can open  

The scientific decisions live in stages 2 and 3. The software job is to make those stages **explicit, logged, and re-runnable**.

## What good looks like

- Failures produce a row-level log, not a full crash with no output  
- Parameters (e.g. molecular weight cutoffs) live in a config file, not buried in code  
- A short validation note states assumptions (“we strip salts”, “we do not enumerate tautomers”)  

## When to wrap it in a UI

If only computational chemists run the pipeline, a CLI may be enough. When medicinal chemists need the same operations weekly, a thin desktop or Streamlit front end often pays for itself — that is the problem Chemistry Companion is aimed at.

If your team is stuck between “I know the chemistry” and “I can maintain the script,” that is a classic consulting engagement boundary.
