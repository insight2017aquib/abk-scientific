Labs lose time on glue work: renaming files, reshaping tables, assembling status reports. Automation helps — until an opaque model changes a scientific decision with no trail.

## A simple rule

**Automate the mechanical. Keep humans on the judgment.**

Good candidates:

- Format conversion and column mapping  
- Batch rename and folder layout  
- Drafting report shells from known templates  

Poor candidates (without checkpoints):

- Interpreting assay anomalies  
- Accepting/rejecting compounds on subtle SAR grounds  
- Anything that must be defended in a regulatory or publication context without logs  

## LLM-assisted steps

Language models can help with triage and drafting **when** inputs/outputs are clear and a human reviews the result. Prefer:

- Structured prompts  
- Logged prompts and outputs  
- Explicit “review required” gates  

## Bottom line

The goal is reliable hours back on a named workflow — not a chatbot demo. Start with a short audit of tasks that are frequent, painful, and mechanical enough to script.
