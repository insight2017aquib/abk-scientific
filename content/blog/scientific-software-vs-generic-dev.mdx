Drug-discovery and research software projects fail more often from **misunderstood science** than from poor React components.

## The translation tax

When requirements pass through product managers who do not read chemistry, you get:

- Dashboards that ignore instrument export quirks  
- “Simple” SMILES fields that break on salts and multi-component systems  
- Automation that looks good in a demo and cannot be audited  

A medicinal chemist who codes reduces that translation tax. Requirements stay in scientific language until they become code.

## What to look for in a partner

- They ask about **file formats and failure modes** before frameworks  
- They distinguish **research prototypes** from **production internal tools**  
- They will tell you when software is *not* the right fix  

## Practical next step

Write down one workflow that costs hours every week: inputs, outputs, who runs it, what “correct” means. That single page is enough to start a useful consulting conversation.
